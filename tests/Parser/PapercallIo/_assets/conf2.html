React London 2017

London, UK

http://react.london

CFP closes on: January 31, 2017 20:00 UTC

Red Badger is launching a new conference focused on React in London for 2017 – we’re calling it React London 2017.

Date: Tuesday 28th March 2017 Format: Single track, full day. Venue: QEII Centre, Westminster, London

The QEII conference centre is a world-class facility overlooking Parliament Square in London, with state of the art AV, surrounded by some of the world’s most recognisable attractions, including the Houses of Parliament and Big Ben, Westminster Abbey and Downing Street. Buckingham Palace is less than ten minutes walk away.

CFP Description

This is a conference about the React eco- system, if you have an idea for a talk we’d love to hear about it. Everyone interested in submitting a talk proposal to React London 2017 is encouraged to do so, regardless of how much experience they have. We welcome proposals from less experienced speakers and will do what we can to help you prepare.

We would like to encourage submissions from individuals who consider themselves underrepresented on stage at technical conferences.

If you are considering submitting a talk, here’s how we can help:

  1. Provide feedback of potential ideas
  2. Connect you with an experienced mentor for your submission or talk
  3. Review your slides or content and provide advice
  4. Find opportunities to practice your talk

There are plenty of opportunities available to speak - we want to hear from you. Email us at hello@react.london

We will be in touch with those who have submitted successful proposals during the first half of February.

Speakers will be required to deliver a run through of their talk with the conference organisers in person or via Skype 2 weeks prior to the event.

Additional guidelines for lightning talks

  1. No live coding or local demo, it must be 100% slides and hosted online or in a pdf.
  2. Slides featuring code and a video of the demo are fine.
  3. Slides of the selected speakers must be submitted 10 days prior to the conference.
  4. A conference ticket is required in order to submit a lightning talk.

Code Of Conduct

TL;DR — Don’t be a Jerk :) All attendees are expected to adhere to the Code of Conduct at all Conference venues. This includes all React London 2017 volunteers and sponsors. Harassment includes, but is not limited to: Verbal comments that negatively target a person in regards to their gender, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, age, or religion. Continued verbal or physical contact with an attendee who has asked you to stop. Sexual images in public spaces Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following. Harassing photography or recording. Sustained disruption of talks or other events. Inappropriate physical contact. Unwelcome sexual attention. Advocating for, or encouraging, any of the above behaviour. In addition to the above, no weapons or illegal substances are allowed in the conference venues at any time. Enforcement Participants asked to stop any harassing behavior are expected to comply immediately.

If a participant engages in harassing behaviour, event organisers retain the right to take any actions to keep the event a welcoming environment for all participants. This includes warning the offender or expulsion from the conference with no refund. Event organisers may take action to redress anything designed to, or with the clear impact of, disrupting the event or making the environment hostile for any participants. Reporting If someone makes you or anyone else feel unsafe or unwelcome, please report it as soon as possible. Conference staff can be identified by t-shirts. Harassment and other code of conduct violations reduce the value of our event for everyone.
